KASARGOD

Previous Next

Kasaragod district is one of the 14 districts in the southern Indian state of Kerala. Kasaragod was a part of the South Canara district of the erstwhile Madras Presidency. It then became a part of Kannur district of Kerala. Kasaragod was declared as a district on 24 May 1984.The district is bordered to the south by Kerala's Kannur district, and to the southeast and north by Karnataka's districts of Kodagu and Dakshina Kannada respectively. To the east, the district is bordered by the Western Ghats, while along the west, it is bordered by the Laccadive Sea. The district, covering an area of around 1992 km2, has a population (2017 census) of 1,307,375. It has four taluks, namely, Kasaragod, Hosdurg, Vellarikundu and Manjeshwaram Taluk. It has three municipalities: Kasaragod, Kanhangad, Nileshwaram and 38 Gram panchayats.

TOP PLACES TO VISIT

Bekal Fort: Witness The Glory & Grandeur Of Bekal’s Delight

Snuggled in the lap of nature, Bekal Fort is a historic fort surrounded by a beautiful beach that offers captivating views of the Arabian Sea. It was built almost 300 years ago and is considered as one of the largest forts in Kerala. Hence, exploring the beautiful walkway, well-lit beach, and the impressive construction of the Bekal Fort is surely one of the many things to do in Bekal, Kerala.

Nityanand Ashram Caves: Experience Tranquility

In your Bekal things to do itinerary, don’t forget to add Nityananda Ashram Caves which are a group of forty-five caves located in the heart of Hosdurg. Popularly known as Nityananda Ashram, the main attraction of these caves is the sculpture of Swami Nityananda, which is made of panchaloha. Exemplifying skilled craftsmanship and recognized as an international spiritual center, these caves are known to have been carved out of a single rock that features six entrances.

Chandragiri Fort: Walk Through The Glorious Past

Spending some time at the Chandragiri Fort may be one of the unconventional things to do in Bekal but it indeed a must try. Dating back to the 17th century, the fort lies in the Kasaragod District of north Kerala and is protected under State Archaeology Department. Full of inventive and historical significance, this place is visited by locals as well as tourists to enjoy the spectacular views of the estuarine that the River Payaswini forms with the Arabian Sea.

Valiyaparamba Backwaters: Ideal Place To Relax & Unwind

To experience the magical waves of the Bekal backwaters, clouds, and the stunning landscapes, it is a must for every traveler to cruise on the backwaters of Valiyaparamba. So for those who’re wondering what to do in Bekal, exploring this exciting and absolutely delightful Valiyaparamba Backwaters would just be a perfect experience.
